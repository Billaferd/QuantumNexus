# **Quantum Nexus: Detailed Rules**

## **Note to Players**

This rulebook is a comprehensive guide to playing Quantum Nexus, providing the foundational rules for the game. While this rulebook aims to be complete, Quantum Nexus is an evolving game. Quantum Nexus is made of many interconnected systems and it may take a while to learn it. There is a Quantum Nexus Quick Start Guide, to ease new players into the game with a limited rule set. When those are understood, we recommend moving on to this rule book for the full experience.

## **I. Introduction to Quantum Nexus**

### **A. Game Overview**

Quantum Nexus is an exhilarating sci-fi trading card game that transports players into a world of strategic battles between advanced civilizations. Players build decks of cards representing various units, leaders, and powerful events to outmaneuver their opponents. The game requires strategic thinking, tactical decision-making, and a deep understanding of card interactions to achieve victory.

Quantum Nexus can be played by two or more players, with two being the most common configuration. In multiplayer games, players are typically arranged in a circle. A **Game Round** consists of a Start of Round step, followed by an **Activation Sequence** where players take single actions, or turns, in clockwise order until all pass.

The objective of Quantum Nexus is to establish total dominance over the front line, forcing your opponent into a retreat and securing victory via **Frontline Supremacy**.

### **B. Components**

To play Quantum Nexus, you will need the following:

* **A Quantum Nexus Deck:** A customized deck of cards constructed according to the deck-building rules.  
* **A Game Board or Playmat:** To organize cards and track game state (Front Row, Back Row, Deck, Discard, Mission Area).  
* **Tokens:** To represent various game effects (damage counters, resource tokens, status indicators like "Suppressed").  
* **A Randomizer:** A coin or die to determine the starting player.  
* To play Quantum Nexus, each player needs a deck of cards (built according to the deck building rules) and a way to track their Command Points (CP) and Unit Impact Damage. Players will also need tokens to represent various game states and generated units.  
* **The Supremacy Tracker**: Players must share a highly visible, physical tracker (such as a central dial, dice, or a track on a playmat) placed between them. This tracker records each player's individual Supremacy Points (used to determine the winner) and the Combined Supremacy Score (used to determine the current Theater Level).

### **C. The Golden Rule**

If the text on a card directly contradicts the rules in this book, the card text takes precedence.

## **II. Card Types**

There are several types of cards in Quantum Nexus, each with a unique role to play in your strategy:

* **Units:** The soldiers, creatures, and machines you command. Units are the primary method of controlling the board and attacking your opponent. They have **Attack**, **Defense**, and **Range** statistics, along with a **Command Point (CP)** cost.  
* **Leaders:** Powerful, unique units that represent the commanders of your forces. They behave like units but often have game-changing abilities. A deck is limited in how many Leader cards it can contain and only one leader can inhabit a quadrant at one time.  
* **Assets:** Structures, equipment, or locations that stay on the battlefield to provide persistent benefits, resource generation, or defensive buffs. They typically do not attack and are deployed to the special Loadout Area.  
* **Events:** Single-use cards that generate an immediate effect—such as dealing damage, drawing cards, or modifying game rules—and are then discarded.  
* **Upgrades:** Enhancements that attach to a specific Unit or Leader to boost their stats or grant them new keywords. If the host unit is destroyed, the Upgrade is destroyed as well.  
* **Terrain:** Cards that modify the battlefield itself. When played, they occupy a specific Quadrant, providing cover, hazards, or strategic bonuses to any unit occupying that space.  
* **Missions:** Special cards that sit outside your main deck. They are the chronological blueprint for your strategy. Mission Cards dictate your base Command Point (CP) income, your deck-building restrictions (Domain Points), and your late-game power scaling via the Theater System. A Mission Card's per-round CP salary is inversely proportional to its late-game power; cards with devastating Theater Level 3 abilities will provide a heavily taxed, lower early-game CP income.

## **III. Deck Construction**

Constructing a legal deck is the first step to victory. Quantum Nexus uses a unique "Point-Buy" system rather than a card count limit.

### **A. The 200 CP Limit**

Your main deck (comprising Units, Leaders, Assets, Events, Upgrades, and Terrain) must contain cards with a maximum **Command Point (CP)** value of **200**.

* There is no minimum or maximum number of cards, the card costs themselves will determine the deck size.  
* You may choose to play fewer high-cost cards or many low-cost cards, as long as the total is no more than 200\.  
* Only 25 points can be assigned to leader cards.

### **B. Leader Limit**

Within your 200 CP budget, you may spend no more than **25 CP** on cards with the **Leader** subtype. This prevents a player from fielding an army consisting entirely of commanders.

### **C. Mission Cards**

You must select upto 6 Mission Cards. These are separate from your main deck and do not count toward the 200 CP limit.

Legality (DP): Mission Cards provide Domain Points (DP) associated with the four domains (Technology, Magic, Psionics, Divinity). The total DP provided across all of your chosen Mission Cards must equal exactly 6\. Furthermore, no single domain can accumulate more than 4 DP. To include a card in your main deck, the card's DP requirement must be less than or equal to the total DP provided by your Missions for that domain.

Mission Cards are divided into three escalations governed by the global Theater Level:

* Logistics (Theater Level 1): The sum of the CP values on your Mission Cards determines your base CP income at the start of every round.  
* Escalation (Theater Level 2): Unlocks the passive ability printed on the card when the global board reaches 8 combined Supremacy Points.  
* Climax (Theater Level 3): Detonates the one-time, game-ending reward printed on the card when the global board reaches 14 combined Supremacy Points.

### **D. Sideboarding**

To mitigate matchup polarization and raise the strategic ceiling of competitive play, a standardized sideboard is a core part of the game's design.

* **CP Limit:** The total CP value of the sideboard must be at most **40 CP**.  
* **Rules:** All cards in your sideboard must adhere to your deck's Mission DP requirements. Between games in a match, you may swap cards from your sideboard into your main deck, ensuring your main deck remains at most 200 CP.

## **IV. The Battlefield**

The battlefield is divided into a grid for each player, creating a structured tactical environment.

* **Front Row:** The primary combat zone. Units in the Front Row can attack units in the enemy's Front Row. Holding the Front Row is essential for victory.  
* **Back Row:** The deployment and support zone. Units typically deploy here. They are safer from melee attacks but must move forward to engage or use ranged attacks.  
* **Quadrants:** Each row is divided into three zones: **Left**, **Center**, and **Right**.  
  * **Adjacency:** A unit is considered "adjacent" to units in the quadrants directly next to it (horizontally and vertically).

## **V. Gameplay Structure**

### **A. Setup**

1. **Place Missions:** Players reveal their Mission cards and place them in the Mission Zone.  
2. **Determine First Player:** Flip a coin or roll a die.  
3. **Draw Hands:** Each player draws 5 cards.  
4. **Mulligan:** A player may shuffle their hand back into the deck and redraw 5 cards once.

### **B. Game Round**

The game is played in a series of rounds, each consisting of two phases.

1\. Start of Round Step  
All players perform these steps simultaneously:

* **Refresh:** Ready (untap) all exhausted cards, remove temporary tokens, and set CP totals to zero.  
* **Gain CP:** Gain Command Points equal to the total CP value of your Mission Cards.  
* **Draw Cards:** Draw cards until you have 5 cards in your hand. (If you already have 5, do not draw).  
* **Resolve Triggers:** Any effects that happen "At the start of the round" resolve now.

2\. Activation Sequence  
This is the core phase of the game, characterized by alternating actions.

* **Initiative:** The player with Initiative takes the first action. (In the first round, this is the player who won the coin toss. In subsequent rounds, it is the player who was the first to pass in the unbroken pass sequence that ended the last round).  
* **Actions:** Players take turns performing **ONE** single action. The turn passes to the next player after the action resolves.  
  * **Deploy:** Pay the CP cost to play a Unit, Upgrade, Asset, or Terrain card from your hand. Units must be deployed to the Back Row unless they have a keyword stating otherwise. Assets are always deployed to the Loadout Area and Upgrades must be deployed onto units.  
  * **Move:** Move a unit one quadrant to an adjacent friendly quadrant (e.g., Back-Left to Front-Left, or Front-Center to Front-Right), the unit is immediately exhausted after the movement.  
  * **Attack:** Exhaust a unit to attack an enemy unit within range.  
  * **Activate:** Use an "Active Ability" on a card already in play (indicated by the text "Action: \[Effect\]").  
  * **Play Event:** Pay the CP cost to play an Event card from your hand. Resolve its text and discard it.  
  * **Pass:** Choose to do nothing.  
* **End of Sequence:** The Activation Sequence ends only when **all players pass consecutively**.  
  * *Strategic Note:* If you pass, and your opponent takes an action, you may take an action again when the turn returns to you. You are only locked out of the round if everyone passes.  
  * The player who passed *first* in an unbroken sequence of passes to initiate the end of the round gains **Initiative** for the next round.

**3\. The Scoring Step:** Immediately following the consecutive passes that end the Activation Sequence, a formal Scoring Step occurs. Both players evaluate the positional state of the Front Row. A player earns one (1) Supremacy Point for every friendly Front Row quadrant physically occupied by at least one unit, or indirectly controlled via spatial projection or action-taxing illusions, that directly faces an unoccupied enemy Front Row quadrant.

* Theater Escalation: After points are tallied, check the combined total of both players' Supremacy Points. If the combined total reaches 8, the game enters Theater Level 2, and all players immediately activate the passive abilities on their Mission Cards. If the combined total reaches 14, the game enters Theater Level 3, and all players immediately resolve the Climax abilities on their Mission Cards.

## **VI. Combat Mechanics**

Combat in Quantum Nexus is deterministic, rewarding positioning and calculation over randomness.

### **A. Initiating an Attack**

To attack, choose one of your **Ready** units and **Exhaust** it. Select a valid target based on your unit's Range.

* **Close Range:** Can only target enemies in adjacent quadrants (enemy Front Row units directly in front).  
* **Mid Range:** Can target adjacent quadrants as well as the diagonal quadrants touching the unit's current quadrant.  
* **Far Range:** Can target any unit in the row that is one row away. If the unit is in the back row it can target any quadrant in the opposing front row. If the unit is in the front row then it can target any quadrant in the opposing back row.  
* **Far Range Override**: If a Far Range unit occupies the Front Row and an enemy unit occupies the directly opposing Close Range quadrant, the Far Range unit may declare an attack against that Close Range enemy. When executing this Close-Quarters Override, the attacking Far Range unit suffers a \-1 penalty to its Attack value for the duration of the combat resolution.

### **B. Resolution**

Compare the **Attack** value of the attacker to the **Defense** value of the defender.

1. **Attack \> Defense:** The target is **Destroyed** and placed in the discard pile.  
2. **Attack \= Defense:** The target is **Suppressed**. It is Exhausted (if ready) and receives a "Suppressed" token. While Suppressed, a unit cannot activate abilities or attack. If a Suppressed unit is the target of a subsequent valid attack, it is immediately destroyed, provided the secondary attacker possesses a base Attack value of 1 or greater. Attacks originating from units with an Attack value of 0 are ineffective and cannot trigger the destruction of a Suppressed unit.  
3. **Attack \< Defense:** The attack is ineffective. The target remains unharmed.  
* Note: if a unit is in the back row they get a \+1 to their defense stat.

### **C. Damage & Persistence**

Unless a card explicitly uses damage counters (like the **Impact** keyword), units do not retain damage. They are either destroyed, suppressed, or unharmed.

* **Impact:** If an attacker has Impact, it places permanent damage counters on the defender equal to the Impact value, regardless of whether the attack initially destroys the target. Each Impact counter currently on a unit acts as a continuous \-1 modifier when calculating that unit's Effective Defense. If a unit's Effective Defense is reduced to 0 or below at any time due to Impact counters, the unit is immediately destroyed. When resolving standard attacks, the attacker's Attack value is compared directly against the defender's newly reduced Effective Defense.

## **VII. Winning the Game**

The game ends immediately when a player accumulates ten (10) Supremacy Points during the Scoring Step, representing an irreversible collapse of the opponent's military infrastructure. If multiple players reach 10 Supremacy Points in the same Scoring Step, the player with the highest total score wins. If tied, the game is a draw.

If any player runs out of cards in their deck then they are eliminated immediately.

## **VIII. Continuous Improvement**

Deck building is an ongoing journey. The metagame evolves, new cards are released, and your understanding of the game deepens. Continuously test, refine, and adapt your deck based on your experiences and the ever-changing landscape of Quantum Nexus.

## **Appendix A: Standard Token Dictionary**

When a card instructs you to create a specific token, use the following statistics. Do not create custom tokens without explicit reference to this list.

* **Construct (Technology):** 1 Attack / 1 Defense. No keywords. *Mechanical infantry used for holding ground.*  
* **Spirit (Divinity):** 2 Attack / 2 Defense. Flying, Ethereal. *Manifestations of pure faith.*
* **Echo (Psionics):** 0 Attack / 1 Defense. Illusion (Dies if targeted). *Psychic projections meant to confuse the enemy.*  
* **Golem (Magic):** 2 Attack / 1 Defense. No Keywords. Magical infantry for offensive assaults.  
* **Scrap (Neutral):** 0 Attack / 1 Defense. Immobile. *Debris used as improvised cover.*
