## Narrative Deck Architecture & Rules Integration

The illustrative table above provides the raw mathematical data for four distinct Quantum Nexus core archetypes. However, under the updated mechanics of the fluid frontline, action parity, and strict combat layering, these raw numbers dictate highly specific operational narratives. The following prose analyzes the composition of each deck, translating the tabulated data into actionable, rules-compliant strategy.

### The Technology Fortress (200 RP)

The Technology deck focuses on systemic resilience and overwhelming, persistent firepower. The strategic contract is established by exactly two Mission cards: the 4 Domain Point (DP) **Singularity Engine** (Technology) and the 2 DP **Blessed Logistics** (Divinity). Neither mission consumes Draft RP or Runtime CP intrinsically, but they establish the 4/2 DP legality limit for the deck.

The infantry line consists of three **Trench Engineer** units (1 DP, 3 RP/CP) and four **Vanguard Phalanx** units (2 DP, 4 RP/CP). A critical element of the updated rules engine is that Phalanx formations rely strictly on their *printed base Defense* rather than their effective Defense after Impact counters are applied. This ensures the Phalanx can endure initial volleys but remains susceptible to the persistent accumulation of Impact. To mitigate this attrition, the deck runs three **Field Medic Automaton** constructs (1 DP, 3 RP/CP) alongside three **Zealous Chaplain** infantry units imported from the Divinity domain (2 DP, 4 RP/CP). The inclusion of the Chaplains introduces the Zeal keyword, which now explicitly triggers *only after* all other survival checks—including Phalanx—are fully resolved, providing layered, mathematically sound defense.

For heavy armor, the deck fields three **Heavy Siege Crawler** vehicles (3 DP, 7 RP/CP) and two colossal **Titan-Class Mech** vehicles (4 DP, 12 RP/CP). Deploying a 12 CP Titan leaves the player with a mere 3 CP from their 15 CP universal base salary; however, utilizing the new 5 CP Reserve banking system allows the architect to carry over unspent Command Points, facilitating massive 20 CP rounds where the Titan can be deployed with CP left over for reactionary tactics. Furthermore, Massive units like the Titan-Class Mech now operate under the updated Quadrant Damage rule, capping their simultaneous targeting to a maximum of two units per attack, ensuring opponent infantry formations are not entirely invalidated. 

The deck establishes absolute infrastructural dominance through a suite of Assets: two **Aegis Defense Batteries** (Fortification, 2 DP, 5 RP/CP), three **Artillery Emplacements** (Weapon, 2 DP, 6 RP/CP), one **Command Bastion** (HQ, 4 DP, 10 RP/CP), and two **Construct Fabricators** (Factory, 3 DP, 8 RP/CP). The Artillery Emplacements benefit immensely from the updated Line of Sight mechanics; friendly units and friendly-occupied quadrants never obstruct friendly ranged attacks, meaning the Vanguard Phalanxes can securely screen the Contested Zone without blinding the backline artillery.

Finally, three **Targeting Uplink** upgrades (1 DP, 3 RP/CP) and a suite of tactical Events supplement the defense: three **Suppression Fire** tactics (2 DP, 4 RP/CP), three **Kinetic Barrier** tactics (1 DP, 2 RP/CP), two apocalyptic **Orbital Strike** tactics (4 DP, 8 RP/CP), and three **Holy Aegis** spells from Divinity (2 DP, 4 RP/CP). Orbital Strikes serve as the necessary board clear, functioning properly within the Threat-Answer matrix by demanding significant CP expenditure.

### The Magic Storm (200 RP)

The Magic deck relies on volatile tempo manipulation, sequence chaining, and graveyard recursion via Arcane Recall. The deck's legal framework is set by the 4 DP **Temporal Fracture** Mission (Magic) and the 2 DP **Arcane Repository** Mission (Magic), cementing a pure 6/0 mono-domain strategy.

The infantry presence is intentionally lightweight, serving primarily as magical conduits. It runs three **Aether Channeler** units (1 DP, 3 RP/CP), three **Rift Mage** units (2 DP, 5 RP/CP), and three **Mindweaver** units splashed from Psionics (1 DP, 4 RP/CP). To provide necessary bulk, two **Arcane Colossus** constructs are fielded (3 DP, 8 RP/CP). 

The infrastructural backbone is highly focused on resource generation and spell enhancement, fielding three **Leyline Nexus** generators (2 DP, 6 RP/CP) and two **Cerebral Network** HQs imported from Psionics (2 DP, 7 RP/CP).

The true lethal potential of the deck resides in its vast Event repository. The arsenal includes four **Aetheric Blast** spells (2 DP, 4 RP/CP), three massive **Reality Fracture** spells (3 DP, 8 RP/CP), three **Psychic Tether** tactics from Psionics (1 DP, 3 RP/CP), four **Mana Surge** spells (1 DP, 2 RP/CP), two **Erase from Time** spells (3 DP, 8 RP/CP), two **Archmage's Mantle** magic cards (2 DP, 4 RP/CP), two **Supernova Catalyst** spells (4 DP, 8 RP/CP), two **Cognitive Dissonance** tactics (2 DP, 4 RP/CP), and one **Mental Shatter** tactic (2 DP, 4 RP/CP).

A critical inclusion is the three **Echo Projection** tactics from Psionics (1 DP, 2 RP/CP). These generate Echo tokens which adhere to the comprehensively updated Illusion keyword. Echo tokens uniquely enter the battlefield ready but are prohibited from moving or taking actions on the turn they enter. When targeted by an attack, the Illusion keyword forces the opponent into a targeting tax—the Echo shatters before the attack resolves, but the attacker's action is *not* consumed, forcing them to select a new valid target rather than locking them into an un-interactive action-denial loop. If the Magic player falls 4 or more Supremacy Points behind while assembling their complex Arcane Recall sequences, the Reinforcement Draw mechanic automatically grants them one additional card during the Draw Step to re-stabilize their tempo. Furthermore, any Theater 3 Climax abilities unlocked by Temporal Fracture must now be proactively executed as an action during the *following* round's Activation Sequence, allowing the opponent an interactive window to brace for impact.

### The Psionics Web (200 RP)

The Psionics deck is an architecture of absolute psychological warfare, precision removal, and action denial. Its legal foundation relies on the 4 DP **Psionic Supremacy** Mission (Psionics) and the 2 DP **Automated Defenses** Mission (Technology). 

The vanguard forces consist of four **Cerebral Inquisitor** units (2 DP, 4 RP/CP), three **Neural Architect** units (2 DP, 5 RP/CP), and three **Synaptic Executioner** units (3 DP, 5 RP/CP). These infantry units frequently exploit the Skirmisher keyword to engage in hit-and-run tactics; under the new strict action parity rules, a unit utilizing a free Skirmisher reposition remains exhausted and cannot be readied by any card effects for the remainder of the Game Round, preventing infinite kiting loops. Support and heavy removal are provided by three **Scrap Warden** constructs from Technology (1 DP, 4 RP/CP), two **Synaptic Nullifier** vehicles (3 DP, 8 RP/CP), and two **Leviathan-Class Null-Mech** vehicles (2 DP, 12 RP/CP). Like all Massive units, the Leviathan-Class Null-Mech's Quadrant Damage is mechanically capped at two targets.

Infrastructural fortification is achieved through three **Automated Barricade** fortifications from Technology (2 DP, 4 RP/CP) and two **Panopticon Relay** HQs (2 DP, 6 RP/CP).

The Event suite is designed to strip the opponent of agency and hand advantage. The deck fields two **Ego Collapse** spells (2 DP, 3 RP/CP), one **Memory Wipe** tactic (3 DP, 7 RP/CP), three **Industrial Conscription** tactics from Technology (1 DP, 3 RP/CP), one devastating **Psychic Scream** spell (4 DP, 8 RP/CP), three **Precognitive Dodge** tactics (1 DP, 3 RP/CP), two **Thought-Theft** spells (2 DP, 5 RP/CP), two **Induced Betrayal** spells (3 DP, 5 RP/CP), two **Telekinetic Eviction** tactics (2 DP, 4 RP/CP), and two **Sensory Deprivation** spells (2 DP, 3 RP/CP). The strategic execution of 5 CP Reserve banking is vital for the Psionics player, allowing them to stockpile resources to cast a 5 CP Thought-Theft and an 8 CP Psychic Scream in a single rapid sequence to instantly shatter an opponent's carefully constructed strategy. 

### The Divinity Crusade (200 RP)

The Divinity deck is engineered for relentless attrition and inevitable scale, turning sacrifice into spatial dominance. The strategic contract is forged by the 4 DP **Absolute Crusade** Mission (Divinity) and the 2 DP **Standard Spellcraft** Mission (Magic). 

The infantry core is massive, fielding four **Initiate of the Order** units (1 DP, 3 RP/CP), four **Sanctified Battle-Priest** units (2 DP, 4 RP/CP), three **Reliquary Guard** units (2 DP, 5 RP/CP), and three **Choir of the Ascended** units (3 DP, 6 RP/CP). These units rely heavily on the Adapt keyword. To prevent deterministic, infinite mathematical scaling, the Adapt trait is strictly hard-capped to trigger only the *first* time a unit survives combat each Game Round. The heavy assault is led by two **Seraphic Vanguard** vehicles (4 DP, 10 RP/CP). When utilizing the Swarm keyword to flood the Contested Zone, the Divinity player must adhere to action parity constraints: any secondary unit deployed via Swarm enters the battlefield exhausted. Similarly, when launching a Horde attack, the player may only exhaust ready units that were *not* deployed during the current round.

Infrastructural support is provided by three **Cathedral of Martyrs** fortifications (2 DP, 5 RP/CP) and two **Altar of Zeal** HQs (3 DP, 7 RP/CP). To ensure they can rapidly claim 3-point bursts from Strategic Directives without early-game exploitation, the units must strictly begin the Game Round already occupying the Contested Zone (Row 3) before they are legally permitted to execute the Secure Objective action.

The deck's Event and Upgrade package provides necessary recursion and spatial manipulation. It utilizes four **Righteous Fury** spells (1 DP, 3 RP/CP) and two **Mass Ascension** spells (4 DP, 8 RP/CP). From the Magic domain, it imports four **Aetheric Repulsion** spells (2 DP, 4 RP/CP), four **Arcane Barrage** spells (2 DP, 4 RP/CP), four **Illusionary Bypass** tactics (1 DP, 2 RP/CP), and two **Aetheric Shift** tactics (1 DP, 3 RP/CP). Finally, three **Holy Vow** upgrades (2 DP, 4 RP/CP) are applied to core infantry, ensuring the crusade endures the grueling attrition of the fluid frontline.
