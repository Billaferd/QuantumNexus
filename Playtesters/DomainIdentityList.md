# Quantum Nexus: Domain Identity & Mechanical Validation Protocol

Evaluating domain identity requires moving beyond subjective aesthetic alignment and rigorously measuring the efficiency of isolated mechanical loops. The efficacy of a domain's identity is directly correlated to its ability to manipulate the 15 Command Point (CP) economy, the 5 CP Reserve, and the spatial grid according to its specific design parameters. The following validation metrics must be aggressively tested to ensure each domain operates exclusively within its intended strategic paradigm.

## I. The Technology Domain: Spatial Denial and Attrition

The core identity of Technology relies on constructing an unbreachable physical wall, grinding down opposing forces through 'Impact' accumulation, and protecting high-value Assets. Validation must confirm that the domain achieves victory through board stagnation rather than explosive tempo swings.

* **Phalanx and Steadfast Integrity:** The 'Phalanx' mechanic must strictly prevent the destruction of units prior to base Defense depletion, and the 'Steadfast' keyword must adequately prevent opponent-forced spatial displacement. Measure instances where Massive enemy units successfully bypass this physical blockade.
* **Asset Economy:** Verify that players are successfully utilizing the 'Singularity Engine' Mission to subsidize the start-of-round CP upkeep of Assets like 'Construct Fabricator' and 'Command Bastion'. Without this subsidy, the domain's action economy should demonstrate a severe deficit.
* **Construct Token Engine:** Measure the action-economy viability of generating Small Size (1-Weight) Construct tokens. These tokens must provide sufficient physical blockades against advancing units, forcing the opponent to expend CP on removal rather than forward momentum.
* **Impact Accumulation:** 'Impact' counters must effectively serve as an alternative win condition by systemically degrading high-defense units over multiple rounds. Track whether Impact damage is successfully lethal without requiring traditional attack actions.

## II. The Magic Domain: Temporal Resource Exchange and Spatial Manipulation

Magic's identity is defined by rule subversion, high-cost Event chaining, and dynamic temporal scaling. This domain must strictly operate as a combo-oriented engine that sacrifices early-game physical presence for catastrophic late-game execution.

* **Reactionary Casting Efficacy:** When utilizing the 'Temporal Fracture' Mission, the +1 CP penalty for out-of-sequence Event casting must be appropriately balanced against the severe tempo disruption it causes the opponent.
* **Terrain Manipulation:** The 'Null-Magic Zone' and 'Aetheric Storm' Terrain cards must successfully partition the 5x3 grid into distinct zones of asymmetrical advantage, protecting fragile units from targeted removal.
* **Event Recursion and CP Sinks:** Players must be able to successfully cycle high-impact Events like 'Reality Unravels' using the 'Chronomancer' and 'Arch-Magus Vael'. Evaluate whether the 12 CP cost of the board wipe completely paralyzes their Activation Sequence or if Reserve banking allows for follow-up actions.
* **Evasion over Defense:** Magic players must rely adequately on the 'Skirmisher' keyword and 'Temporal Loop' bounce effects to survive, validating their physical fragility in the Vanguard row.

## III. The Psionics Domain: Information Asymmetry and Systemic Degenesis

The Psionic identity demands absolute control over the opponent's options. It does not win physical engagements; it prevents them from occurring. Validation must ensure that hand disruption and action denial do not cross the threshold into uninteractive, guaranteed lockouts.

* **Synaptic Mark Economy:** The setup-to-payoff ratio of the 'Synaptic Mark' state must function seamlessly. Applying the mark via units like 'Neural Architect' must directly enable low-CP executions via 'The Silent Judge' or 'Synaptic Executioner' without consuming the entire 15 CP budget.
* **Hand Disruption Thresholds:** Monitor the frequency of empty opponent hands. Cards like 'Cerebral Inquisitor' and 'Matriarch Liana' must degrade the opponent's options without causing a complete mathematical forfeiture of the match by round three.
* **Action Denial and Parity:** 'Exhausted' and 'Suppressed' states generated by 'Psychic Scream' or 'Mental Collapse' must successfully disrupt the alternating Activation Sequence without violating the fundamental rules of engagement.
* **Illusion Token Interactivity:** The 'Echo' tokens utilizing the 'Illusion' keyword must strictly force attack redirection rather than providing an absolute, un-targetable shield that blanks opponent combat phases.

## IV. The Divinity Domain: Inevitable Crusade and Value Recursion

Divinity's identity is built on self-fueling attrition, where the destruction of friendly units generates immediate economic or physical advantages. Validation must ensure that 'Zeal' and 'Martyrdom' loops do not result in infinite stalling.

* **Zeal Thresholds:** The 'Zeal' keyword must trigger exclusively after 'Phalanx' thresholds and base Defense are entirely depleted, effectively doubling the required action expenditure for the opponent to permanently remove the unit.
* **Martyrdom Engine Balance:** Measure the CP and card advantage generated by 'Spawning Pool' and 'Cathedral of Martyrs' upon unit destruction. These engines must strictly maintain parity; they cannot allow the Divinity player to snowball an insurmountable economic lead early in the match.
* **Recursion Speed:** Players must efficiently utilize 'Resurrection Rite' and 'Altar of Zeal' to repeatedly deploy 'Sanctified Battle-Priest' and 'Zealous Chaplain' directly to the Contested Zone.
* **Swarm Action Parity:** Deploying multi-unit formations via the 'Swarm' keyword must strictly adhere to the rule that secondary units enter the battlefield exhausted, definitively preventing single-turn overwhelming strikes from an empty board state.
