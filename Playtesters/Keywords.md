# Universal Keyword & Status Effect Glossary

The mechanics of Quantum Nexus rely on a precise vocabulary to govern interactions across the highly fluid 5x3 spatial grid. Understanding these keywords is critical for mastering the Threat-Answer matrix and optimizing the 15 CP universal economy. In accordance with the transition to the Fluid Frontline model, Flanking Crossfire has been permanently removed to streamline combat, while size classifications have been updated to reflect the new 3-Weight visual capacity system and their corresponding Native Traits. 

Below is a comprehensive, detailed explanation of every keyword, status effect, and size classification in the game, presented first in narrative prose to establish context and mechanics, followed by an illustrative table for quick reference.

### Comprehensive Keyword Explanations

**Adapt:** A unit with Adapt thrives on surviving conflict. Whenever this unit survives combat, it gains a permanent +1/+1 counter, increasing its base Attack and Defense statistics. 

**Aetheric Shielding:** This keyword provides a strong economic defense against spellcasting. Any opponent attempting to target this unit with an Event card must pay a mandatory tax of 3 additional Command Points (CP) added to the Event's normal runtime deployment cost.

**Arcane Recall:** A signature mechanic of the Magic domain that turns the discard pile into a weapon. You may play this card from your discard pile by paying its runtime CP deployment cost and fulfilling any additionally specified resource costs. Once a card played via Arcane Recall resolves, it is exiled from the game rather than being returned to the discard pile.

**Armor Piercing:** This keyword allows attacks to bypass normal mathematical damage calculations. When calculating combat resolution, damage dealt by this card or effect ignores the target's printed Base Defense, acting as a binary threshold bypass rather than a standard comparison.

**Ethereal:** This represents ghostly, incorporeal resilience. Combat damage dealt to this unit during standard attacks is automatically reduced by 1.

**Flying:** Airborne units ignore the chaos and congestion on the ground. This unit completely ignores physical Line of Sight obstructions (quadrants currently at maximum 3-Weight capacity) when determining valid targets for attacks within its numeric Range.

**Horde:** This keyword rewards mass mobilization and overwhelming numbers. When taking an Attack action during the Activation Sequence, your units with the Horde keyword occupying the same quadrant can attack and exhaust as a single action.

**Illusion:** A fragile trick of the mind utilized heavily by Psionics. If a unit with this keyword becomes the explicit target of any enemy attack, activated ability, or Event, the unit is immediately destroyed prior to the resolution of that effect. If destroyed by an attack declaration, the attacker's action is fully consumed and the attacking unit exhausts.

**Immobile:** This unit is physically rooted in place. It cannot be chosen as the valid target of a standard Move action or a Tactical Advance, and it is entirely immune to being relocated by any card effects from either player.

**Impact:** Impact represents persistent, incremental damage accumulation rather than a floating mathematical modifier. When an effect inflicts Impact on a target, or a unit survives a non-lethal attack, an Impact counter is placed on it. Each Impact counter represents 1 point of persistent damage. If a unit's accumulated Impact counters ever equal or exceed its Defense value, the unit is immediately destroyed.

**Infiltrator:** These units strike behind enemy lines, disrupting standard formations. You may deploy this unit directly into your Vanguard (Row 2). This overrides the general baseline rule that units must be strictly deployed to your Deployment Zone (Row 1).

**Large:** This size classification defines frontline linebreakers. This unit's physical size possesses exactly 2 Weight. Because every quadrant is governed by a strict visual limit of 3 Weight, a single quadrant can safely hold one Large unit and one Small unit. Large units possess the Native Trait of **Pushback**: When a Large unit successfully inflicts an Impact counter on a defending unit, it may force that unit to retreat into an empty adjacent quadrant. If no empty quadrant exists, the defender suffers 1 additional Impact counter instead.

**Martyrdom:** This represents power found in sacrifice, heavily utilized by the Divinity domain. When this unit is destroyed, immediately trigger the associated ability text.

**Massive:** This size classification defines overwhelming titans. This unit's physical size possesses exactly 3 Weight, completely filling a quadrant's capacity upon deployment or movement. A quadrant containing a Massive unit claims absolute spatial dominance and acts as a physical Line of Sight obstruction that blocks ranged attacks from passing through it. A quadrant may only ever contain 1 Massive unit. Massive units possess the Native Trait of **Quadrant Damage**: When a Massive unit declares an attack against a target quadrant, its Attack value is compared against the Defense of every unit in that quadrant simultaneously.

**Momentum:** This universal game rule keeps the frontline violently fluid and rewards decisive action. If an attacking unit completely clears a target quadrant of all enemy units, it may immediately move into that newly cleared quadrant as part of the same Activation at no additional CP cost.

**Nullify:** A strategic weapon against recursion. Cards destroyed by this unit's attacks are exiled from the game instead of being placed in the discard pile, permanently preventing Arcane Recall or Martyrdom synergies.

**Phalanx:** This represents defensive strength found in tight proximity. If this unit is adjacent to at least one other friendly unit that also possesses the Phalanx keyword, it cannot be destroyed by an attack unless the Attack value exceeds its Defense by 2 or more. This shifts the defensive bonus from a dynamic mathematical calculation to a strict, binary survival threshold.

**Phantom:** These units are masters of obfuscation. This unit cannot be chosen as a valid target for standard attacks unless the attacking unit is positioned in an orthogonally adjacent quadrant (Range 1).

**Skirmisher:** This keyword enables hit-and-run tactics. After this unit performs an Attack action, you may immediately move it to an adjacent friendly-occupied or empty quadrant (orthogonally, or diagonally if it is a Small unit). This special maneuver does not consume an additional action for your turn during the Activation Sequence.

**Small:** This size classification represents agile infantry and tokens. This unit's physical size possesses exactly 1 Weight. A maximum of 3 Small units can safely occupy a single quadrant. Small units possess the Native Trait of **Evasion**: Small units are agile flankers permitted to move diagonally across the grid. Additionally, Small units do not block friendly Line of Sight for ranged attacks.

**Steadfast:** Unmovable resolve. This unit cannot be forcibly moved or pushed back from its current quadrant by an opponent's card effects or Native Traits.

**Swarm:** This allows for rapid, overwhelming board flooding. When taking a "Deploy" action to play a unit with the Swarm keyword, you may simultaneously deploy exactly one additional unit with the Swarm keyword from your hand into the exact same quadrant. You must pay the individual runtime CP costs from your 15 CP universal salary for all units deployed in this manner.

**Synaptic Mark:** The signature Psionic setup tool. The mark on its own does nothing, but it is used to legally target the afflicted unit with other powerful, domain-specific abilities and Events.

**Taunt:** This keyword forces unwanted engagements. Enemy units that declare an attack must target a unit with Taunt if one is within their numeric range and constitutes a valid target based on Line of Sight. If there are multiple enemy units with Taunt available, the attacking player may choose which one to target.

**Territorial:** This provides strict area denial. A unit with this keyword actively blocks enemy deployment, standard movement, and Tactical Advances into any orthogonally adjacent quadrant, locking down physical firing lanes on the 5x3 grid.

**Zeal:** A display of divine resilience. The first time this unit would be destroyed in a given Game Round, it instead survives, removes all accumulated upgrades and Impact counters, and immediately becomes exhausted.
